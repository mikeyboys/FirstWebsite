Excel vs. SharePoint Lists: Which is Right for Your Business Data?

3 September 2025, by STAFFAPPS Australia

In countless businesses, Microsoft Excel isn't just a program; it's practically a core member of the team. It holds the project plans, the customer lists, the safety register, and, most importantly, the office footy tipping comp. But we've all lived the chaos that follows when one spreadsheet holds too much power. It begins with an office-wide hunt for the one true version of a file, usually named something like `ClientData_FINAL_v4_JensEdits_FORREAL_USETHISONE(2).xlsx`. Then comes the dreaded pop-up: "This file is locked for editing by Dave," who, of course, went on lunch an hour ago. This daily drama of file-wrangling and praying a fragile spreadsheet doesn't corrupt begs the question: is there a better way? For many, the answer is SharePoint Lists.

Where Excel Still Excels

Let's be clear: Excel is a phenomenal tool and isn't going anywhere. It is the perfect choice for tasks that involve heavy calculation, data analysis, and visualisation. If you're doing financial modelling, creating complex charts for a report, or working on a dataset by yourself to find insights, Excel is your best friend. Its power lies in its flexibility for a single user to crunch numbers and analyse data.

Think of Excel as a brilliant workbench for an individual craftsperson. It’s perfect for detailed, one-off jobs.

Where SharePoint Lists Shine

If Excel is a workbench for one, a SharePoint List is a collaborative assembly line for a team. It's designed from the ground up to be a central, secure, and structured place for your team's data. Here’s why it’s often the better choice for your operational data:

True Multi-User Collaboration: This is the biggest difference. Multiple people can view and edit a SharePoint List at the exact same time without ever locking the file or creating conflicting copies. Everyone is always looking at the one single source of truth.

Data Integrity and Structure: In SharePoint, you can define what kind of data goes into each column (e.g., Text, Date, Choice, Person, Number). This prevents users from entering "ASAP" into a due date column or mistyping important information, ensuring your data is clean, consistent, and reliable.

Version History and Security: SharePoint automatically tracks the history of every single item in your list. You can see who changed what and when, and even restore a previous version. You can also set granular permissions, giving certain people read-only access while others can edit, something that's very difficult to manage in a simple Excel file.

The Perfect Partner for PowerApps: This is the crucial point for building business apps. SharePoint Lists are a native data source for PowerApps. This means they are fast, reliable, and work seamlessly together. Building an app on a SharePoint List is significantly more robust and scalable than trying to build one on an Excel spreadsheet stored in a shared drive.

Choosing the Right Tool for the Job

Trying to run a shared business process from an Excel file is like using a screwdriver to hammer a nail; you might get it done, but you're making life a lot harder than it needs to be! The rule of thumb is simple: for individual data analysis and calculations, stick with the power of Excel. But the moment that data needs to be shared, edited by a team, and used as a reliable foundation for a business process or a PowerApp, it's time to move it to a SharePoint List. Migrating your data is often the first, most important step towards building a truly efficient business application.
